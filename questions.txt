  aq2_e = s*xs*ys

  So even when called with ebeam = E_1 - E_\gamma (ISR case):                                                                   
  - $E_2$ is correctly reduced: aEe2 = ebeam*(1-y) = (E_1-E_\gamma)(1-y) ✓

  This means for ISR the code builds:                                                                                           
  - Reduced $E_2 = (E_1 - E_\gamma)(1-y)$ ✓

  The scattered electron angle is then forced to satisfy Born $Q^2$ with reduced $E_2$:                                         
  acosthe = (2*E1_rc*E2_rc - 2*me² - Q²_Born) / (2|p1_rc||p2_rc|)                                                               
                                                                                                                                
  This gives a different angle than Born — but the $Q^2$ assigned is the Born value, not the true ISR $Q^2$.                    
                                                                                                                                
  ---                                                                                                                           
  The correct ISR treatment should be:                                                                                          
                                                            
  When the ISR photon takes $E_\gamma$ from the beam, the hard vertex sees:
  $$k_1' = k_1 - k_\gamma, \qquad Q^2_\text{true} = -(k_1' - k_2)^2$$                                                           
                                                                                                                                
  The sampled $(Q^2, y, t)$ are the Born values. For ISR they should be interpreted as the kinematics at the hadronic vertex, so
   the true beam at the vertex is $E_1' = E_1 - E_\gamma$, and the scattered electron should have:                              
  $$E_2 = E_1'(1-y), \qquad Q^2_\text{vertex} = E_1' \cdot E_2 \cdot y \cdot \text{factor} \neq Q^2_\text{Born}$$               
                                                                                                                                
  Practical impact for our kinematics:                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                
  $E_\gamma/E_1 \approx 40\text{ MeV}/33\text{ GeV} \approx 0.1%$ — the error is tiny.                                          
                                                            
  So the strategy is physically correct to ~0.1% for our narrow kinematics. The approximation breaks down only for large $v$    
  (large $E_\gamma$).
